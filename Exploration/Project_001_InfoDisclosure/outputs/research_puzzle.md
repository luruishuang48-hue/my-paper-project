# Research Puzzle

**Date:** 2026-06-15  
**Stage:** 1 — Puzzle Refinement

---

## Project Context Used For Refinement

The raw input is sparse, so this stage uses the existing project materials as context. The empirical project studies 60 AI model releases and 86 AI-related listed firms. The current strongest result is that the Artificial Analysis Intelligence Index predicts CAR[0,+20] mainly for closed-source releases. Narrower coding and math indexes are weaker. Open-weight releases weaken the capability-return slope. These statements are project-internal empirical findings, not newly verified external facts.

---

## Central Research Question

When a public AI model release reveals a higher capability signal, under what conditions does that signal raise the market value of AI-exposed firms rather than diffuse into a general technology spillover with weak private rents?

---

## Central Tension

**Tension:**  
AI capability is public information once a model is released, but the cash-flow value of that information depends on whether the releasing ecosystem can appropriate the rents. A higher benchmark score can either create proprietary profit opportunities or accelerate industry-wide diffusion that erodes private value.

**Why neither side obviously wins:**  
The same technical signal can have opposite valuation implications. If access is controlled through APIs, cloud contracts, proprietary products, or platform lock-in, capability is a positive rent signal. If weights or know-how diffuse broadly, capability also informs competitors and downstream adopters, weakening the link between quality and the releasing ecosystem's private value. This makes the sign and magnitude of the stock-market response non-obvious.

---

## Core Mechanism

**Causal chain:**  
Public capability disclosure with an appropriability regime → investors update expected private rent capture versus diffusion spillovers → abnormal returns of AI-exposed firms respond differently across closed-source and open-weight releases

**Expanded description:**  
The minimal theory should treat model capability as a public signal about a latent productivity or demand shifter. The key friction is not that investors fail to observe the signal, but that the mapping from technical capability to cash flows depends on appropriability. Closed-source releases make the signal closer to private rent news. Open-weight releases make the same signal partly a public input, increasing spillovers and reducing the releasing ecosystem's marginal valuation response. This structure can also explain why a broad intelligence index matters more than narrow coding or math measures if investors value the general-purpose rent base rather than a task-specific benchmark.

---

## What a Complete Answer Requires

A complete paper addressing this question would need to establish:

1. A valuation equation in which a firm's market value depends on expected AI-related rents, and those rents depend on both capability and appropriability.
2. A comparative static showing that the price response to a capability signal is larger when the appropriability parameter is high.
3. A diffusion result showing that open-weight release lowers private rent capture even when it raises aggregate technology availability.
4. A metric-relevance result showing why a broad capability signal can dominate narrow task-specific signals when firms monetize general-purpose AI demand.

---

## Welfare Dimension

**Is there a conflict between private incentives and social optimality?**  
Yes, but it should be secondary. Closed-source capability may generate stronger private valuation because rents are captured, while open-weight capability may create larger spillovers with weaker private valuation. Private market value and social diffusion value can therefore diverge.

**The normative question:**  
The welfare comparison is between proprietary rent capture and open diffusion. The empirical paper need not claim welfare, but a theory appendix could show why stock-market reactions are not social-value measures.

---

## Primary Economic Phenomenon

**What this paper illuminates about how economies work:**  
The theory would clarify why capital markets may price technological progress selectively. A public technical breakthrough is not automatically private cash-flow news. It becomes private valuation news when institutions, contracts, intellectual property, or platform control make the new capability appropriable. This is especially relevant for frontier AI because model releases simultaneously reveal quality, alter competitive expectations, and determine how broadly the technology diffuses.

---

## Main Difficulty

**The hardest part of answering this question:**  
The difficulty is conceptual and identification-facing rather than mathematical. A simple model can easily assume that closed-source means higher appropriability and then mechanically produce the observed comparative static. To be useful, the model must deliver at least one non-tautological implication, such as a sign reversal when diffusion benefits competitors more than the releasing firm, or a ranking of broad versus narrow capability signals.

---

## Triviality Check

**Would the answer be trivially yes or no without a model?**  
The weak version is trivial: if closed-source firms capture more rents, markets value capability more. The non-trivial version is to show when a public capability signal increases private value despite revealing information to rivals, and when open diffusion can produce high social value with low or even negative private stock-market response. The model must therefore include both rent capture and spillover channels.

---

## Refined Hypothesis

**The paper's working hypothesis (to be confirmed or refuted by the model):**  
The stock-market slope on AI capability is increasing in appropriability because investors price expected private rent capture, while open diffusion weakens or reverses the slope by converting capability news into a public input for competitors and downstream firms.

---

## Scope Boundaries

**What this paper IS about:**
- A stylized information-economics and innovation-rent model linking public AI capability signals to private market valuation.
- The difference between technical capability as a social technology signal and capability as an appropriable cash-flow signal.
- A theoretical rationale for the empirical focus on closed-source releases and broad capability measures.

**What this paper is NOT about:**
- A full equilibrium model of the AI industry.
- A welfare ranking of open-source versus closed-source AI as a policy conclusion.
- A structural asset-pricing model with risk premia and stochastic discount factors.
- A theory proving that the existing empirical estimates are causal.

---

## Preliminary Viability Assessment

There is a viable theory model, but it should be modest. The best candidate is a two-channel disclosure model with capability and appropriability. It can support the empirical paper by giving discipline to three facts: broad capability matters more than narrow benchmarks, closed-source releases have the strongest valuation slope, and open-weight releases weaken that slope. It should not be sold as a standalone theory paper unless later stages produce a genuinely non-trivial proposition beyond the monotone appropriability comparative static.

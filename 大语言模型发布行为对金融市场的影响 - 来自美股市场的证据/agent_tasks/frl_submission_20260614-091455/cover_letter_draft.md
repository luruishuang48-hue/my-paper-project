# Cover Letter Draft

Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, “The Market Pricing of Proprietary AI Model Capability,” for consideration at *Finance Research Letters*.

The paper studies which technical capability measures equity markets price after major AI model releases. We assemble 60 model-release events from 2024 to early 2026, match them to 86 AI-related listed firms, and compare Artificial Analysis Intelligence, Coding, Math, and Media Elo measures. The main result is concise. The broad Intelligence Index carries the strongest long-window pricing signal, while narrower coding and math measures do not show the same pattern. For closed-source releases, a one-standard-deviation increase in the Intelligence Index is associated with a 3.0 percentage point higher CAR[0,+20]. The relation is weaker for open-weight releases.

The contribution fits the short-format style of *Finance Research Letters*. The paper connects event-study evidence with the economics of appropriability in a setting where public AI benchmarks reveal information about commercially important technological progress. The evidence is strongest for broad proprietary capability, survives firm fixed effects, alternative abnormal-return models, event-level aggregation, leave-one-creator-out tests, and small-cluster inference, and is not mirrored in pre-event returns.

The manuscript is original, has not been published, and is not under review elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission.

Sincerely,

Zhuo Chen
